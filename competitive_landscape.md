# Competitive Landscape

## Reflection

**What makes Focus Bear different from these apps?**

    Focus Bear is different because it fills the gap that all other apps miss. Freedom and Cold Turkey are great for blocking distractions, but they don't help with actually building good habits or providing structure to your day. Tiimo and Routinery are good for building habits, but they don't have the same level of distraction blocking features. Focus Bear combines both habit-building and distraction-blocking in one app, which makes it a more comprehensive solution for people who struggle with focus and time management. Additionally, Focus Bear's design is more user-friendly and visually appealing, which can make it more engaging and easier to use compared to some of the more utilitarian apps in this space.

**If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?**

    As a user, I would choose Focus Bear over competitors because it offers a more holistic approach to improving focus and productivity. The combination of habit-building features and distraction-blocking tools means that I can not only eliminate distractions but also develop better habits that support my goals. Focus Bear is more proactive since it takes over the screen and guides you through your habits. For someone who struggles with "knowing what to do but not doing it," having an app that essentially enforces your best intentions is a huge advantage. Additionally, the fact that Focus Bear is designed with a specific target audience in mind (people who struggle with focus and time management) makes it feel more tailored to my needs compared to more general productivity apps.

**What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t?**

    Tiimo has a very strong visual countdown and icon-based interface that is great for people with severe time blindness. It makes it very easy to see how much of a task is left at a glance. Freedom also has better cross-device syncing, allowing you to start a block on your Mac and have it automatically apply to your phone and tablet at the same time. Focus Bear currently lacks these features, which could be a drawback for users who rely heavily on visual cues or need seamless syncing across multiple devices.

**Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?**

    One major improvement would be a native Google Calendar integration. Right now, a lot of people manage their lives through their calendars. If Focus Bear could automatically sync with a user's calendar, it could force-start focus blocks between meetings or automatically trigger a wind-down routine after the last event of the day. This would save the user from having to manually set up their routines every time their schedule changes, making the app more seamless and integrated into their daily life. Additionally, it would allow Focus Bear to provide more personalized recommendations based on the user's schedule, further enhancing its value as a productivity tool.
