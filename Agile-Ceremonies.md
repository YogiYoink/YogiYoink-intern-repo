# Agile Ceremonies

## Reflection

**How do Agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?**

    Agile ceremonies create a predictable rhythm for communication. They help with alignment by reducing information silos just like how daily stand-ups ensure everyone knows what others are working on. Agile ceremonies also help by creating feedback loops like retrospectives, which allow honest discussions about team health, preventing small issues from becoming major problems. They also help clarify goals from planning and refinement that ensures the team is working on the most high-value tasks.

**Which ceremony do you think is most important for your role, and why?**

    For my role as a UX Design Intern, I think Continuous Prioritization (Kanban) is the most important ceremony  for me. It allows me to ensure that the design work I’m doing is always aligned with the most current business goals and user needs. It also allows me to adapt quickly to changes in project scope or user feedback, which is crucial in a fast-paced environment. By participating in continuous prioritization, I can make sure that my design efforts are focused on the most impactful features and improvements, ultimately contributing to a better user experience.

## Task

**Document one change you can make to improve your team collaboration.**

    One change I can make to improve team collaboration is to proactively share my design work and seek feedback from cross-functional team members early and often. This means not waiting until a design is perfect before sharing it, but rather treating it as a living document that can evolve based on input from developers, product managers, and other stakeholders. This way, I can catch potential issues early, ensure that my designs are feasible and aligned with technical constraints.
