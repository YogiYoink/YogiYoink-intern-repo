# UX Prioritization

## Reflection

**If a PM tells you there’s no time for a UX improvement, how would you respond?**

    I would try to find a middle ground. Instead of giving up, I’d ask, "If we can't do the full redesign now, can we implement a quick fix (like changing a hex code) to reduce the immediate pain for users?" I would also ask to add the larger improvement to the Backlog so it isn't forgotten in the next sprint. I’d also try to understand the PM’s constraints and priorities, and then frame the UX improvement in terms of how it can help achieve those goals. For example, if the PM is focused on increasing user retention, I could explain how improving the user experience can lead to higher satisfaction and loyalty, ultimately benefiting the business. I’d also offer to help with the implementation to make it easier for the PM to say yes.

**How can UX designers ensure that important usability issues don’t get ignored?**

    The best way is to maintain a UX Debt Log. Just like developers have "Technical Debt," we should track "UX Debt." By showing a growing list of friction points, the team can see that small annoyances are starting to pile up and affect the overall quality of the product. I would also prioritize issues based on their impact on user experience and business goals, and communicate these priorities clearly to the team. Regularly sharing user feedback and data can also help keep usability issues top of mind for everyone involved in the project. Additionally, I would advocate for regular UX reviews and testing sessions to identify and address usability issues before they become bigger problems.

**What’s the best way to present UX work as a business priority rather than a "nice-to-have"?**

    I would frame the UX work in terms of ROI (Return on Investment). For example, regarding the problem that I found where tasks disappear when you make changes, fixing this isn't just about polish, it reduces support tickets and prevents new users from uninstalling the app out of frustration within the first 5 minutes. When UX is tied to user retention, it becomes a business necessity. I would also use data and user feedback to demonstrate the impact of UX improvements on key metrics such as conversion rates, customer satisfaction, and retention. By showing how UX work can directly contribute to the success of the business, I can help shift the perception of UX from a nice-to-have to a critical component of the product strategy. Additionally, I would collaborate with stakeholders to align UX goals with overall business objectives, ensuring that everyone understands the value of investing in user experience.
