# AI Usage Guidelines

## Reflection

**When should you use AI for assistance, and when should you rely on your own skills?**

    I will use AI for repetitive tasks like brainstorming varied layout structures, generating realistic "lorem ipsum" replacements, and creating initial icon sets. However, I will rely on my own skills for high-level empathetic research, making final aesthetic decisions, and conducting live usability testing where human intuition and observation are irreplaceable.

**How can you avoid over-reliance on AI while still benefiting from it?**

    I will follow what I've been doing which is a "Human-First" design process that requires sketching my initial concepts on paper or a digital whiteboard before looking at AI-generated wireframes. This ensures my creative discovery is driven by my own creative imagination and problem-solving skills rather than simply selecting from AI-suggested templates which are typically reused from already published templates.

**What steps will you take to ensure data privacy when using AI tools?**

    I will follow a strict "Sanitize Before Input" rule, removing all specific project names, company identifiers, or real user personas before asking an AI for help with a design problem. I will also check the privacy settings of any Figma plugins to ensure they are not using my work to train their public models.

## Task

**Identify one task you can improve using an AI tool, and try it out.**

    I used an AI tool to generate a sitemap and initial wireframe structure for a specific feature flow of a personal project that I had in mind.

**Review the AI-generated output critically—did it require editing or fact-checking?**

    While the AI provided a logical starting point, it missed critical user touchpoints unique to this project's technical context. I had to manually edit the flow to ensure it properly addressed the specific needs of the target audience.

**Document one best practice you will follow when using AI tools at Focus Bear.**

    I will follow the "Sanitize Before Input" rule that I have mentioned earlier, which means no specific company names, internal project titles, or real user data will ever be shared with AI tools to maintain the highest level of data privacy.
