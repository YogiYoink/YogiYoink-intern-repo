# Learning Goals

## Reflection

**What do you hope to learn from this internship?**

    I hope to learn how UX design actually functions in a real-world startup environment, specifically for a product that helps people with neurodiversity. I want to see how user feedback is turned into actual design changes and how to balance "cool" design ideas with the practical needs of users who might easily get overwhelmed by too much visual noise.

**What skills are you most excited to develop?**

    I’m really excited to get better at Figma for professional hand-offs and to dive deeper into User Research. I've been working on my design skills, but I want to move beyond just the visual side of design and understand the "why" behind it like learning how to simplify a user’s journey so they don't get overwhelmed or lost while using the app.

**What do you think you can contribute to the team?**

    I can bring a fresh perspective to the design team. I’m used to working on projects that require a lot of attention to detail and logic, so I can help create designs that are clean and easy to follow. I’m also a quick learner and ready to help out with any research or prototyping that needs to be done to move a feature forward.

**What is one area you feel less confident in that you’d like to improve?**

    I’d like to get better at UX Writing. Finding the right words for buttons or instructions is harder than it looks, and I want to learn how to guide users with clear, friendly language instead of making things sound too complicated or stiff. I think improving my UX writing skills will help make the app more approachable and easier to use for everyone, especially for those who might already feel overwhelmed by too much information.

**What does success in this internship look like for you?**

    Success for me means finishing my 540 hours with a solid portfolio of work that I’m proud of. More importantly, I want to feel confident that the designs I worked on actually made the app easier to use for someone struggling with their daily routine. I want to end the internship feeling like a much more well-rounded designer. I also hope to build strong relationships with the team and learn how to collaborate effectively in a startup environment. Overall, success would be knowing that I contributed to making Focus Bear a better product while also growing a lot as a designer.
