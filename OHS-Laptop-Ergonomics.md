# OHS Laptop Ergonomics

## Reflection

**What equipment changes can you make to improve your workspace setup? (e.g., using an external monitor, adjusting your chair, using a laptop stand)**

    I will prioritize using a laptop stand. Raising the laptop to eye level is the most immediate way to fix my posture. Since I can't type comfortably on a raised laptop, I will also integrate an external keyboard and mouse. This separation of viewing and typing is essential for a sustainable workflow.

**What behavioural changes can you implement to improve posture and reduce strain? (e.g., sitting upright, taking regular breaks, adjusting screen height)**

    I will implement a check-in habit wherein everytime I start a new task in Figma, I will consciously roll my shoulders back and check if my ears are aligned over my shoulders. I will also adopt the 20-20-20 rule to reduce eye strain where every 20 minutes, I will look at something 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds. This allows the eye muscles to relax after staring at a backlit screen for prolonged periods. Finally, I will avoid leaning the head toward the screen by ensuring my font sizes in my design tools are large enough to read comfortably from an arm's length away.

**How can you remind yourself to maintain good posture and take breaks throughout the day? (Hint: Use Focus Bear to schedule movement breaks!)**

    I will use Focus Bear to automate my breaks. I have a tendency to enter a flow state where I forget to move for hours, which is when the most physical damage occurs. I will schedule mandatory movement breaks every 25 minutes. When the reminder pops up, I will perform a quick routine like standing up, doing stretches to open up my chest, and rolling my wrists. Automating these breaks ensures that my physical health is managed with the same consistency as my project deadlines. This will also force me reset my posture before it becomes a problem

My external keyboard is placed at a height where my forearms are parallel to the floor, significantly reducing the tension in my upper back.

    The most critical piece of equipment I’ve identified is a Vertical Ergonomic Mouse. As a UX designer, my day involves thousands of precise clicks. A vertical mouse keeps the forearm in a handshake position, which prevents the bones in the forearm from crossing and reduces pressure on the median nerve.
