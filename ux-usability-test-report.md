# UX Usability Test Report

## Report Summary

    I conducted a usability test with a friend unfamiliar with Focus Bear and the scenario was that he's a university student trying to use the app to evaluate the initial setup of a "Deep Work" session and the blocking feature. While the participant found the session setup intuitive, the Blocking feature failed to function as expected, leading to a significant drop in user trust and clarity. The participant expressed frustration and confusion when attempting to access blocked websites, which did not trigger the expected blocking behavior. This issue overshadowed the otherwise positive experience of setting up a session, highlighting a critical area for improvement in the app's functionality and user experience.

## Key Findings/Feedback to Questions

    1. The participant noted that setting up the "Deep Work" session was straightforward and intuitive. However, confusion arose during the configuration of the "Blocking" section, as the websites added to the list were not successfully restricted.
    2. The participant reported a positive effect on their concentration. They highlighted that the organized to-do list and the dedicated timers for breaks and focus provided a strong sense of structure and motivation.
    3. The primary source of frustration identified was the "Blocking" section's failure to function, specifically the inability of the app to restrict access to the websites specified in the user's list.
    4. The participant described the overall experience using three words: Helpful, Structured, Confusing.
    5. The core confusion centered on the functional logic of the "Blocking" section. The participant expected the app to enforce restrictions immediately upon adding a URL to the list, which did not occur.
    6. The participant explicitly stated that the expected website blocking behavior did not happen during their session.
    7. The participant recommended an immediate enforcement of blocks once a website or app is added to the list. Furthermore, they suggested that the Windows application UI for the "Blocking" section should be redesigned to be more organized, structured, and user-friendly.

## Conclusion

    In conclusion, this test proves that while Focus Bear is great at providing a motivating structure for students, the tech needs to keep its promises. People love the to-do list and the timer, but when the blocking feature doesn't kick in immediately, that sense of control disappears. By fixing the hidden logic and making the Windows UI more responsive, we can turn that initial frustration into the seamless, high-trust experience that users are looking for.
